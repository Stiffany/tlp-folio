My UX workflow

Empathise - to help define the problem, Define, Ideate, Prototype - learn from prototypes to spark new ideas, Test - tests create new ideas for the project

We all have our preferred processes and ways of working.

It is essential that Entry level UX designers learn these guidelines and consider aligning their design practices with them.

I've been a senior/Lead UX designer and UI developer for a long time now - cookie cutter processes are useful for a brand new product or service - for the majority the value ad is negligible.

Accessibility being the exception - accessible design benefits everyone from situational accessibility to designing for safety.

98% of the world's top one million websites don't offer full accessibility. WebAIM conducted a study in 2020 that found the vast majority of websites do not offer a fully accessible experience. Or, put another way, less than 2% of the world's top one million websites are capitalizing on the disability market.

Empathise & Define

When possible I like to create personas. This is how i demonstrate a thorough understanding of who my end users are and what they want to achieve. If your going to perform this task then share your work with your team, product owners and some of your stakeholders. Refer to them by name and recognise what task & interactions align to their individual needs and goals.

Story boarding

The term Story board was borrowed from the movies, graphic design & advertising industry. It’s just a comic strip that is exactly like a comic book in that it outlines the main characters actions and circumstances under which these actions are performed. The power is that it demonstrate what the user is doing, and it also reveals the environment, which might be affecting how or why the user does or doesn’t do something.

It's been my experience fitting engaging storytelling into the UX process via research findings, abstract design concepts, team alignment, design ideas and implementation and adjusting those stories to the audience you are presenting to is far more beneficial.

Customer Journey maps

A customer journey map (also known as an experience map) is a diagram that represents the steps (i.e., the process) taken by a user to meet a specific goal. "The customer journey is the complete sum of experiences that customers go through when interacting with your company and brand. Instead of looking at just a part of a transaction or experience, the customer journey documents the full experience of being a customer." Essential for new products or services.

Ideation Deliverables, Brainstorming

Brainstorming is a process whereby a team of designers generate ideas about how to address the issues and opportunities identified in the user research phase. The concept here hinges on the generation of as many ideas as possible (even if they are completely wild) so that the designers can later sift through these and reduce them to the ideas that seem most promising. The best solutions can sometimes sprout from the craziest-sounding notions.

High-fidelity prototypes

Pixel-perfect prototypes show all the visual and typographic design details of a digital product and service. When I go into hi-fi prototyping I make sure I have all the final content, headings, form labels, paragraph text & images. I'm not a fan of Lorem Ipsum as its distracting to research participants, product owners and stakeholders. Copy can break a UI therefore it’s important to know the length of your copy. My hi-fi's take into consideration physical screen dimensions and are produced in a size that corresponds to the numerous and growing physical device’s sizes.

When hi-fi prototypes are produced to pixel perfect standards and are fully interactive they are more often than not used to conduct meaningful user testing without the overhead front-end and back-end development, which means the data gathered from user testing can be considered and used to iterate before the product or service is ready to go into development. Epics, Sprints and Stories can be created quickly and with little to or no need for additional discovery or research.

In the morning after stand up I like to spend a few minutes with the front end or back end developer whose picked up a story and make sure they have everything they need to complete the stories and or tasks requirements. There's nothing more annoying than having to chase up requirements. Its a waste of time for the project, business and developer.
