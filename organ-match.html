Summary

Brief: NOMS has been supporting the transplantation sector for the last 16 years, which is an amazing achievement. But technology over that time has substantially shifted and the current platform needed to be replaced.
Blood Service launched of the discovery phase of the Australian Organ Matching System (AOMS) project — a joint initiative between the Organ Tissue Authority (OTA) and the Blood Service — at the Melbourne Processing Centre.
AOMS will replace the current National Organ Matching System (NOMS) and is a new platform to enable the transplantation sector to determine the most appropriate match of solid organs for transplant patients.
